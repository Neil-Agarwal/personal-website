Astratta

As part of a school design project to create an accessible musical instrument, I worked within a team of 6 to design and build an accessible piano for stroke survivors.

One of our main goals was to not only effectively solve the problem we chose to tackle but to maximize the number of users that are affected by our problem space. This is because we wanted our solution to have a big impact. Subsequently, we decided to focus on stroke survivors because it is a common debilitating affliction with roughly 800 000 people experiencing a stroke in the US each year according to the American Heart Association. As for our instrument, we chose the piano because of its popularity and importance in music.   

After thorough research of our problem space, we found that a common effect of stroke and the main culprit for making piano inaccessible for stroke survivors was hemiparesis which results in a loss of strength and dexterity on one side of the stroke survivor’s body. Combining clinical and user research as well as identifying our project constraints, we defined our goal to be to:

“Design an accessible piano to be used by stroke patients with hemiparesis to recreationally make music in a household setting such that they can independently play popular songs like Heart and Soul”.   After iterating on our design and three user testing sessions, our team is proud to have built a functioning medium fidelity prototype that has met our goal in four short months.

I have always been interested in solving complex problems. However, more often than not, complex problems tend to require an inter-disciplinary approach where one needs to understand all the aspects of a system to truly craft a beautiful solution.

This is precisely why I chose to study Systems Design Engineering.

It is a hollistic approach to engineering where students learn to understand all aspects of engineering. Using this knowledge and a big picture approach, they can then solve complex problems which require an understanding of software, electrical, computer, chemical, and management engineering.

With a focus on design methodlogies, Systems Design Engineers are incredible at identifying solutions that not only tackle the problem at hand but are a joy to use. By existing at the intersection of technological ingenuity and design, there is no problem that Systems Design Engineers are not equipped to solve.
