Projects

SydeDoor

SydeDoor is a Ruby on Rails web application that is currently being developed by a team of six University of Waterloo students. The website aims to provide a convenient way for students in co-op programs to post and share their internship experiences with one another as well as allow students to connect with upper-years in their program to receive guidance and mentorship.

By creating an online platform where knowledge about co-op can be shared among students, the SydeDoor team is hoping to better the experience of all students in the co-op program by demystifying the co-op process so that students can make the most out of their internships.

Education

University of Waterloo

Bachelor of Applied Science, Systems Design Engineering
I have always been interested in solving complex problems. However, more times than not, complex problems tend to require an inter-disciplinary approach where one needs to understand all the aspects of a system to truly craft a beautiful solution.

This is precisely why I chose to study Systems Design Engineering.

It is a hollistic approach to engineering where students learn to understand all aspects of engineering. Using this knowledge and a big picture approach, they can then solve complex problems which require an understanding of software, electrical, computer, chemical, and management engineering.

With a focus on design methodlogies, Systems Design Engineers are incredible at identifying solutions that not only tackle the problem at hand but are a joy to use. By existing at the intersection of technological ingenuity and design, there is no problem that Systems Design Engineers are not equipped to solve.
